Institutional Act:

Acts which can be strategic or communicative

A primary effect is to shift the legitimacy and influence of an institution

Can either be positive or negative


Examples:

Accreditation

Exposing corruption

Philosophic Act:

Acts which can be strategic or communicative

A primary effect is the transmission of beliefs

Can be ontological, phenomenological, moral, etc.


Examples:

Refusal of vaccine/medical procedure

Choosing Linux over Windows

The Vulgar Discourse

The aggregate of discourse outside of Institutions

Not a formalized, structured discourse

No formalized, combined goal or message

Impossible to understand as a whole, must be segmented

Understanding Vulgar Discourse

Most common approach: Surveys

Extracting repeated phrases or actions

For example: "This large Facebook group repeatedly brings up the 'Jewish Question' or 'Pizzagate'"

Analysis of social overlaps

For example: Noticing that members of a political group follow similar news accounts on social media
